Explain quantum entanglement in simple terms, then search for latest breakthroughs
Quantum entanglement is when two particles become connected in a way that defies classical physics. Once entangled, measuring one instantly affects the other—even across vast distances. Einstein famously called this "spooky action at a distance."

Key points:
• The particles aren't secretly deciding their states beforehand
• The connection isn't limited by light speed (information still can't be sent faster than light)
• It's one of quantum mechanics' most verified phenomena

Recent breakthroughs: Scientists just achieved entanglement over 44 km of fiber optics (2024), moving toward practical quantum networks. Read more →
What are the practical applications?
Entanglement powers three game-changing technologies...

1. Quantum Computing: Entanglement lets qubits process exponentially more information than classical bits.

2. Quantum Cryptography: Theoretically unhackable communication—any attempt to intercept is immediately detected.

3. Quantum Sensors: 1000x more precise measurements (gravitational waves, atomic clocks).
